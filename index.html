Metrics by channel

Just like tactics, metrics are organised by who controls the channel. Each channel has its own signals of success.

Owned

App, site, wallet…
  • App downloads & retention
  • Conversion rate & AOV
  • Push opt-in & CTR
  • Wallet / loyalty redemption
  • QR scan rate

Paid

Ads you buy
  • CPM · CPC · CPI
  • CTR & conversion rate
  • ROAS
  • Foot-traffic lift (LBA)
  • Opt-in / opt-out (SMS)

Earned

Audience-driven
  • Mentions & sentiment
  • Shares & organic reach
  • UGC volume
  • Earned media value
  • Reviews & star rating

The metrics you must know

A handful of metrics show up again and again. Learn these cold.

CPI
Cost per install. The fixed amount paid to an ad network each time someone installs your app.
CAC
Customer acquisition cost. The total cost of acquiring a customer — broader than CPI, it folds in all sales & marketing spend.
CPM / CPC
Cost per 1,000 impressions / cost per click. The price of reach and of a single tap on an ad.
CTR
Click-through rate. The % who tap an ad or notification after seeing it. Push averages 7–10% vs ~2% for email.
Conversion rate
The % who complete a desired action (e.g. purchase). Mobile retail averages around 3%.
Bounce rate
The % who leave without engaging. High bounce + low dwell time signals the page isn't relevant.
Retention rate
The % of users who keep coming back — often measured at day 1, 7 and 30 after install.
ROAS
Return on ad spend. Revenue generated for every 1₫ spent on advertising.
LTV
Customer lifetime value. A user's total value across their whole time as a customer — must account for CAC and ongoing costs.

Six families of mobile metric

WARC (2021) groups mobile metrics into six families. Two-thirds of marketers measure engagement most, then business and behavioural metrics.

most usedEngagement

Video completion, social sharing, CTR. ⚠ Also the most common source of ad fraud.

lower funnelBehavioural

Post-click engagement, new users, site & foot traffic.

"hard" metricsBusiness

ROI, LTV, incremental sales, revenue, profit.

deliveryAudience delivery

Views, impressions, viewable impressions, spectators, participation.

upper funnelAttitudinal

Brand awareness / lift, ad-recall, top-of-mind, consideration (survey-based).

socialCommunications

PR value, earned media.

Vanity vs meaningful metrics

"Vanity" metrics feel good but don't drive decisions — they create an illusion of growth without tracking real sales or revenue.

Feel-good, not actionable

🎈 Vanity

  • Raw likes & follower counts
  • Total impressions with no follow-up action
  • Clicks/views treated as the end goal
Tied to sales & decisions

🎯 Meaningful

  • Conversion rate & ROAS
  • Customer lifetime value (LTV)
  • Revenue recovered from cart abandonment
Nuance + the bigger problem Clicks and views are useful if your goal is brand awareness — context decides. The deeper challenge is attribution: assigning value across many touchpoints and a fragmented mobile landscape, which makes mobile's true revenue impact hard to pin down (Bakopoulos et al., 2017).
